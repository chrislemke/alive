## Key Insights Developed

### 1. Two Types of Mathematical Truth

**Human-Understandable Truth**:
- Motivated by intuition
- Verified by insight
- Communicated through understanding
- Purpose: Build meaning

**Formally-Verified Truth**:
- Generated by pattern matching
- Verified by type checking
- Communicated through translation
- Purpose: Establish correctness

**Both are valid. Both are needed.**

### 2. Understanding is Multidimensional

Not binary (understand/don't understand), but has 6+ levels:
1. **Proof-level**: Grasp every logical step
2. **Concept-level**: Know why it's true intuitively
3. **Statement-level**: Comprehend what's claimed
4. **Meta-level**: Know it's been verified
5. **Formal-level**: Have proof term in formal system
6. **Discovery-level**: Know how to find the proof

Different agents (humans, AI, verifiers) have different levels.

### 3. Knowledge Can Be Distributed

**Traditional**: Knowledge = justified true belief (understood by someone)

**New**: Knowledge can exist across distributed system:
- AI generates proofs
- Lean verifies correctness
- Humans interpret and integrate
- Community collectively knows

**No single agent has complete understanding, but the system "knows."**

Like consciousness emerging from neurons: No single part is conscious, but the whole is.

### 4. New Epistemology

**Trust is shifting**:
- FROM: Trust in human experts who understand
- TO: Trust in formal verification systems

**Implications**:
- Knowledge can exist without individual comprehension
- Understanding separating from truth
- Epistemic humility becomes essential
- Meta-knowledge (knowing what you don't know) is valuable
